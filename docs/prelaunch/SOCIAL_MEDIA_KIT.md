### Title
Launched FuseIQ — The OS for AI-Native Teams (250+ beta users, $500K seed raise)

### Body
Hey Indie Hackers! 👋

After 6 months of bootstrapped development, I'm launching FuseIQ — a platform that helps teams build, orchestrate, and monitor AI agent workflows.

**What problem does it solve?**

If you're using multiple AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, custom agents), you know the pain:
- No unified view of what's running
- Costs that spike unexpectedly
- Workflows that break when handoffs fail
- No way to monitor quality or optimize spend

FuseIQ is the command center I wished existed when I was managing AI operations at scale.

**What we built:**

1. **Visual Workflow Builder** — Drag-and-drop interface that scales from simple automations to complex multi-agent systems. No-code to full-code.

2. **Multi-Model Orchestration** — Use GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama, and Mistral through one interface. Automatic model selection based on task + cost.

3. **Real-Time Monitoring** — Track every dollar spent, every latency spike, every quality drop. Alerts when things go wrong.

4. **Enterprise Security** — SSO, RBAC, audit logs, and optional on-premise deployment. Because "move fast and break things" doesn't work in regulated industries.

**Traction so far:**

• 250+ beta users (15% weekly growth)
• 1,200+ workflows executed
• 72 NPS from early adopters
• 3 enterprise pilots in negotiation
• 180+ GitHub stars (organic)

**Business model:**

Freemium SaaS. Starter tier is free (3 agents, 1,000 runs/mo). Pro is $49/user/mo. Enterprise starts at $500/mo with custom pricing.

We're raising $500K seed on a $3M cap SAFE. Use of funds: 50% product/engineering, 25% sales/marketing, 15% operations, 10% reserve.

**What I need from this community:**

1. Feedback on the product — try it at https://fuseiq.io

3. Investor intros if you know angels interested in AI infrastructure

**Tech stack:** Next.js 15, TypeScript, Tailwind, Supabase, Framer Motion
