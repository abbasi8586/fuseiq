## Executive Summary

This guide provides a complete roadmap for establishing and operating **Abbasi Global LLC** in Florida as the legal entity behind FuseIQ v3.0. Florida offers significant advantages for tech startups: no state income tax, business-friendly regulations, strong asset protection, and a growing tech ecosystem.

**Key Benefits of Florida LLC:**
- ✅ No state personal income tax
- ✅ No state corporate income tax (for LLCs taxed as pass-through)
- ✅ Strong charging order protection (single-member LLCs protected)
- ✅ Low annual report fee ($138.75/year)
- ✅ No minimum capital requirements
- ✅ Privacy-friendly (member names not public in most cases)

## Step 1: LLC Formation

### 1.1 Name Reservation & Registration

**LLC Name:** Abbasi Global LLC  
**Filing Authority:** Florida Department of State, Division of Corporations  
**Filing Fee:** $125 (Articles of Organization)  
**Processing Time:** 5-10 business days (expedited available)

**Required Information:**
- LLC name (must include "Limited Liability Company," "LLC," or "L.L.C.")
- Principal office address (Florida address required — use registered agent)
- Registered agent name and Florida street address
- Names and addresses of all members (can be single-member)
- Effective date (immediate or future)

**Online Filing:** [sunbiz.org](https://sunbiz.org)

### 1.2 Registered Agent Requirements

**Florida Law:** Every LLC must maintain a registered agent with a physical Florida address.

**Options:**
1. **Self as Registered Agent** — Use your Florida address (if resident)
2. **Commercial Registered Agent** — Professional service (~$100-300/year)
   - Northwest Registered Agent ($125/year)
   - LegalZoom ($299/year)
   - Incfile (free first year)

**Recommendation:** Use a commercial registered agent for privacy and compliance.

### 1.3 Operating Agreement

**Critical Document:** Even for single-member LLCs, an operating agreement is essential.

**Key Provisions:**
- Member ownership percentages (100% Awais Abbasi)
- Management structure (member-managed vs. manager-managed)
- Capital contributions and distributions
- Voting rights and decision-making
- Transfer restrictions on membership interests
- Dissolution procedures
- Intellectual property ownership (FuseIQ platform, code, brand)

**Template Sources:**
- LegalZoom ($99)
- Rocket Lawyer ($39.99/month)
- Custom attorney drafting ($500-2,000)

## Step 2: Federal Tax ID (EIN)

**Purpose:** Required for banking, tax filing, hiring employees, and business operations.

**Application:** IRS Form SS-4 (free, online at [irs.gov](https://irs.gov))  
**Processing:** Immediate (online) or 4-6 weeks (mail)  
**Cost:** FREE

**Required Information:**
- LLC legal name
- Florida address
- Responsible party name and SSN/ITIN
- Business activity (Software Development / SaaS)
- Number of expected employees (0 initially)
- Fiscal year (calendar year recommended)

**Tax Classification:**
- **Default:** Single-member LLC = disregarded entity (Schedule C)
- **Elect S-Corp:** File Form 2552 for potential tax savings (recommended once profitable)
- **Elect C-Corp:** Only if seeking VC funding or going public

**Recommendation:** Start as disregarded entity, elect S-Corp once annual net income exceeds $60,000.

## Step 4: Florida Tax Compliance

### 4.1 State Taxes

**Florida Corporate Income Tax:**
- LLCs taxed as pass-through: **NO state income tax**
- LLCs taxed as C-Corp: 5.5% on net income
- **FuseIQ (single-member LLC): $0 state income tax**

**Florida Sales Tax:**
- SaaS (software as a service): **Generally NOT taxable in Florida**
- Digital goods: Exempt
- If selling physical products or consulting: 6% + local option

**Annual Report:**
- Due: May 1st each year
- Fee: $138.75
- File at: [sunbiz.org](https://sunbiz.org)
- Late penalty: $400

### 4.2 Federal Taxes

**Self-Employment Tax (15.3%):**
- Social Security: 12.4% (up to $160,200)
- Medicare: 2.9% (no cap)
- Additional Medicare: 0.9% (over $200,000)

**Quarterly Estimated Taxes:**
- Due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Pay via EFTPS ([eftps.gov](https://eftps.gov))
- Estimate: 25-30% of net income

**S-Corp Election Benefits (Future):**
- Split salary/distributions to reduce SE tax
- Potential savings: $5,000-15,000/year depending on income
- Must pay "reasonable salary" before distributions

### 4.3 Tax Deductions for SaaS Businesses

**Fully Deductible:**
- Home office (if used exclusively for business)
- Software subscriptions (GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, Stripe)
- Equipment (laptop, monitors, desk)
- Internet and phone (business portion)
- Travel to conferences/meetings
- Professional development (courses, books)
- Legal and accounting fees
- Marketing and advertising
- Domain names and hosting

**Vehicle:** Standard mileage rate ($0.67/mile in 2026) or actual expenses

## Step 5: Intellectual Property Protection

### 5.1 Trademark Registration

**FuseIQ Brand Protection:**

**Federal Trademark (USPTO):**
- Class 9: Computer software
- Class 42: Software as a service
- Filing fee: $250-350 per class (TEAS Plus)
- Processing: 8-12 months
- Duration: 10 years, renewable

**Florida State Trademark:**
- Additional protection within Florida
- Filing fee: $87.50
- Faster processing

**Recommendation:** File federal trademark immediately. Use LegalZoom ($599 + fees) or attorney ($1,500-3,000).

### 5.2 Copyright Registration

**For Platform Code, Documentation, Content:**
- Automatic upon creation (common law)
- Federal registration: $45-65 per work
- Benefits: Statutory damages, attorney fees, public record
- File at: [copyright.gov](https://copyright.gov)

### 5.3 Trade Secrets

**Protect via:**
- Operating Agreement provisions
- NDA agreements (for contractors/employees)
- Access controls (GitHub private repos, Supabase RLS)
- Document marking: "CONFIDENTIAL — TRADE SECRET"

## Step 6: Contracts & Legal Framework

### 6.1 Essential Contracts for FuseIQ

**1. Terms of Service (ToS)**
- User agreement for platform access
- Limitation of liability
- Subscription terms and cancellation
- Data usage and privacy
- Dispute resolution (Florida law + arbitration)

**2. Privacy Policy**
- GDPR/CCPA compliance (even if US-focused initially)
- Data collection practices
- User rights (access, deletion, portability)
- Cookie policy
- Third-party processors (Supabase, Stripe, Vercel)

**3. SaaS Agreement / Master Service Agreement**
- For enterprise customers
- SLA commitments
- Support terms
- Data security obligations
- Indemnification

**4. Contractor Agreements**
- Work-for-hire provisions
- IP assignment
- Confidentiality
- Non-compete (limited, Florida allows reasonable)

### 6.2 Florida-Specific Legal Considerations

**Florida Non-Compete Law:**
- Must be reasonable in time, area, and scope
- Must protect legitimate business interests
- Must be in writing and signed
- Generally enforceable if reasonable

**Florida Data Breach Notification:**
- Notify affected individuals within 30 days
- Notify Florida Department of Legal Affairs if 500+ affected
- Maintain written incident response plan

**Florida Deceptive and Unfair Trade Practices Act (FDUTPA):**
- Prohibits unfair/deceptive business practices
- Important for marketing claims and pricing
- Private right of action for consumers

## Step 7: Insurance

### 7.1 Recommended Coverage

**1. General Liability Insurance**
- Covers: Bodily injury, property damage, advertising injury
- Cost: $400-800/year ($1M coverage)
- Required by: Some enterprise customers, co-working spaces

**2. Technology Errors & Omissions (E&O) / Cyber Liability**
- Covers: Software failures, data breaches, professional negligence
- Cost: $1,000-3,000/year ($1M coverage)
- **Critical for SaaS:** Customer data breaches, platform downtime

**3. Business Owner's Policy (BOP)**
- Bundles: General liability + property + business interruption
- Cost: $500-1,500/year
- Good for home office setups

**4. Directors & Officers (D&O) Insurance**
- Covers: Personal liability of directors/officers
- Cost: $2,000-5,000/year
- Important once raising funding or adding board members

**Recommended Providers for Tech:**
- Hiscox (specializes in tech)
- Next Insurance (online, fast)
- Embroker (startup-focused)
- CoverWallet (comparison platform)

## Step 9: Hiring & Payroll (Future)

### 9.1 Employee vs. Contractor

**Contractors (1099):**
- No payroll taxes, benefits, or workers' comp
- Must meet IRS independent contractor test
- Issue 1099-NEC if paid $600+ in a year
- Best for: Developers, designers, marketers

**Employees (W-2):**
- Must withhold income tax, FICA, unemployment
- Workers' compensation insurance required
- Best for: Core team, customer support

### 9.2 Payroll Services

**Recommended:**
- **Gusto** ($40/month + $6/employee) — Best for startups
- **ADP** — Enterprise-grade
- **Paychex** — Mid-size businesses

### 9.3 Florida New Hire Reporting

- Report all new hires within 20 days
- File at: [floridajobs.org](https://floridajobs.org)
- Federal requirement: New Hire Reporting Program

## Step 10: Funding & Investment Readiness

### 10.1 Pre-Seed / Seed Preparation

**Entity Readiness for Investors:**
- Clean cap table (100% founder-owned initially)
- IP fully owned by LLC (work-for-hire agreements)
- Clean financial records from day one
- Delaware conversion ready (if VC requires)

### 10.2 Delaware C-Corp Conversion (Future)

**When to Convert:**
- Raising institutional VC (they require Delaware C-Corp)
- Planning IPO or major acquisition
- Complex equity structures (multiple classes)

**Process:**
1. Form Delaware C-Corp
2. Merge Florida LLC into Delaware C-Corp
3. Issue stock to founder(s)
4. Update all contracts and IP assignments

**Cost:** $2,000-5,000 (attorney) + Delaware fees

**Recommendation:** Stay Florida LLC until Series A or institutional interest. Convert then.
